The post-covid world: After their long-term social isolation, people commute to work again. Thousands of cars on the road, spewing emissions and contributing to global warming. The average occupation level of cars during rush hour is just about 1.2 people per car. While congestion is only an immediate effect, the long term damage of motorised individual transport to our planet is immense. But how might we solve this crisis for society - and their mobility?

The context: Why I chose a graduation company in the mobility field

My story with Toogethr started during the pandemic, in which I was spending time Germany and repeatedly noticed, how intense the commute traffic in Germany was compared to the Netherlands. Bottom line: I was looking into creating meaningful impact, either by facilitating the long-term shift to carpooling for commutes or more people on bicycles.

Following my observations and thoughts, I reached out to the Creative Director and agreed on the option to carry out my graduation project there.

Toogethr is a Dutch company, who with their ecosystem offers a suite of solutions for more sustainable commuting:

  • Parking 🅿️  A parking management solution as their core product, allowing ways for managing parking lots as well as making corporate parking accessible to the public
  • Carpool: 🚙👨‍👩‍👧 An application for planning commuting journeys with colleagues and receive rewards
  • Cycles 🚴‍♂️ : An app where colleagues can pair up for cycling from / to work together, track their journeys and receive rewards

The context: Maintain & Revive

In the context of a pandemic, my final graduation project at The Hague University focused on how to combine and maintain the increasingly popular modal choice of commuting by bicycle with the post-covidal revitalization of (hybrid) office life.

Bottom line: Establishing new, sustainable commuting routines while inviting employees to socialise

Since I have always been critical towards creating “yet another app”, as well as keeping the activation threshold as low as possible, my approach was it to create an ecosystem involving multiple people and touchpoint, while keeping the most impactful touchpoint at a low interaction threshold.

The result was a smart bicycle bike bell for the office that allows employees to subtly notify their colleagues that they are biking home with a simple touch.

Proposed ecosystem

The bicycle bell is meant to be located easy to access and visible in the office hallway, connected via slack to colleagues working in the same office space.

When an employee wants to go home, they would leave their desk, ring the bell which would then alert the colleagues.

Another way for colleagues to get notified is selecting a preferred day of the week, time slot and building in which they would receive notifications, to lower distractions.

This allows more sustainable commuting behaviour, paired with the post covid office socialising we all need.

Hardware

Smart bicycle bell, consisting of an ESP32S micro controller, an integrated soldered touch sensor, connected to WiFi and the Slack API.

It was feeling like magic: The moment when the bike bell suddenly came to life as an IoT device and independently started sending messages with generated content to a Slack group - and their members started replying.

Further steps were carried out using GitHub Projects, ensuring a good foundation for an agile software & hardware development in the future.

Software: Connection to Slack

User interaction has been one of the main challenges. Getting employees to cycle is one aspect - but starting a conversation between them is another. So I started to include conversation starters that facilitate the ride home with more or less stranger colleagues.

Software: Standalone Frontend

The idea of relying solely on Slack thus became difficult. It might be one of the most popular office messengers, but some would have to maintain the logic around the API connection for each messenger used by customers to keep the ecosystem running.

I therefore decided to come up with an alternative to it that would be completely independent from the Slack bot and instead take the form of a universal app.

This would allow us to bring notifications either to the smartphone, the browser or to a browser extension on the employees’ computer with only one codebase.
