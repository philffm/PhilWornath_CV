Assignment: Travel expenses of the future

Companies use all kind of methods for their expense management. Concur from SAP is for example a popular app (other expenses below). However will cost between 7,50 - 30€ to process one expense claim. This is due to all forms of authorizations and checks that need to be given.

My role in this project was designing the functionality of the Nexpense app in collaboration with FWDpay and selection of recruited research participants from the target group "occasional business travelers" that fed us with actionable insights along the.

Research aim

The overall context deals with the process of expense management. In the given assignment, I will conduct an overarching research on how current expense management systems work, get aware of the touchpoints in the experience of expense management, common obstacles and constraints, users of those system face in order to draw conclusions about possible chances and thus show up ways to streamline and simplify steps within that expense management process.

Research questions

  • How do different people experience business expenses that are involved in the process? - Positive / negative? What is the exact process? How do people pay their expenses? Do they capture every expense / request more than what was spent? How many people/parties are involved in the process
  • What are different use cases where business expenses are conducted? - Do different kind of expenses result in unforeseen obstacles in the design process? Is it needed or valuable to deliver a tailored design for each type of expense?
  • Would users trust a service like FWDpay as to access their bank transactions?
  • What is the current state of Expense Management – what needs to be fixed and how can we learn from other expense management systems? Pros/Cons/Weaknesses

Research insights

  • User Insight #1: Using an internal booking portal for certain purposes (transport / hotel) can already minimize the amount of manual claims to be made, as the system checks with the policies and automatically processes a claim for the order.

    However, according to my participant this method just feels like a “workaround” as the effort for the remaining manual expense claims is still too high and should be automatized.Source: Interview – A – Employee – Jerome Endres

  • User Insight #2: “Opening a claim for the refund of business expenses feels like begging for money”, as employees need actively request their money back instead of getting a prompt/silent refund. Finally, this impression is supported by the huge waiting time for a single refund. When designing the new app, this feeling should be inverted, so the employee is able to act more autonomously without being depended on the financial department

    Source: Interview – A – Employee – Katharina Pauer

  • User Insight #3: The more complex a manual expense capture process is, the more time and cost-intense it gets and errors are more likely to occur. Therefore, the form for opening a manual claim needs to be easy to understand, guide the user towards his goal and notify him, once requests are declined, information is not matching or not yet complete (upload function, categorization, maybe allocation to a client)

    Source: Interview – A – Employee – Marcella S.

  • User Insight #4: Employees are willing to share there bank transfer information with third parties in order to minimize the amount of paperwork.Source: Lucia Podmanicka shared this insight with me. However, All of my interviews (including c-level & accountant), deliver proof for that insight.
